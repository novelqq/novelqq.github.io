The internet has always been pretty shit. When I started using it extensively as a small 10 year old child circa 2008, I was frequently blasted with gruesome banner ads, porn, viruses, pop-ups, email phishing, soulless commercials and products, and a wide array of graphic images and content not suitable for anyone in particular, but definitely not me, whose access was unrestricted and curiosity unsatiable. My internet speed was dogshit, it taking an agonizing amount of time to load webpages with any sort of heavy video or audio content, and I would often pirate games overnight and wake up to find them not even a third of the way complete. Finding things felt like an absurd pagan ritual where you would first crawl Google's PageRank using carefully chosen keywords, browse the bowels of random forums and webrings, and ultimately get lost in curated walled gardens that were becoming increasingly insular in their culture. The reason for me spending so much time online is probably similar to why many other children, teens, ended up glued to it regardless; yes, it was a game that I enjoyed getting better at, learning about computers and how to navigate around the minefield, but it was also easier than real life, and the small, tantalizing nuggets of gold you would find amongst the shitpile kept you looking for more. If you didn't fit in somewhere in school or a club, if you were an alien, an other, there were countless anonymous internet in-groups that you could attach yourself to by simply spending more time there, by lurking.
My internet journey took me to strange, edgier places, turned me into a weeb, accentuated mental illness, and accumulated for me acquaintances and friends both online and offline that all talked the same internet-poisoned, ever-changing language. Having online cred became such an important group signifier for me that even when I became more popular and comfortable in my high school life (A new-found power that I only used for evil, it should be said), I still spent a large majority of time talking on voice calls, on message boards, sharing and creating memes and blog articles, finding obscure music and otherwise being a huge shut-in in-between my Dr. Jekyll normie outings and hobbies. In college, I attempted a full back half "Aku no Hana"-esque normie transition, doing normal college things like club activities, study groups, drinking, partying, seeing shows, kissing girls, jerking off a dude at Sun Peaks, etc. I ended up starting a "serious" relationship, and between the stress of classes, my friends-only-by-proxy, and my least likeable behaviors, had a certifiable crashout that left me wondering if was even capable of having relationships ever, at all. For over a year, I attended classes alone, wept uncontrollably at my shitty job in the warehouse bathroom at 4 am, I experienced strange, unexplainable medical emergencies that hospitalized me briefly, and my body and face changed so much that neither I or former colleagues could recognize me. I *did* recover from some of that eventually, finding some new friends, reconnecting with older ones. I tried to grow as a person, acknowledging the resentful, anti-social parts of me that prevented me from making real connections. But I also retreated to what was comfortable and stable for me: Cool detachment, distanced irony. Of course, lockdowns came not long after, and given it was the only option, I gleefully sublimated myself to the online world, a place that I still thought was familiar, a place that I could navigate with ease and understand wholly. Obviously, that's not true, I understood almost nothing, and anything I did understand was changing or disappearing outright at every moment.
Covid only accelerated a trend that tech companies have been working towards for decades. Walled gardens, algorithmic feeds, AI content, ragebait, radicalization, a whole population divided and conquered, sequestered and alone. The forces are overwhelming, unstoppable. All the obscurities and internet lore that I internalized as my own have been signal blasted to everyone with a phone. They decide elections. Marginalize groups. Dominate the news. 4chan felt like a secret, but now I see it everywhere. 100 years of /v/ Gamergate. /fit/ broscience podcasts. /mu/ top 100 tiktoks in /fa/ moodboards. /r9k/ crashouts. /pol/ shooters. This world is already fated to end, and our islands are becoming smaller and smaller. I've always been fascinated by Japanese media centered on the internet in the 90s, 00s, denpa stuff, Boogiepop, Welcome to the NHK, Serial Experiments Lain, Lily Chou Chou, Peep Show and so on, and I think now I understand why, it's because their highly connected internet, economic recession, and the Subway Sarin Attack caused them to immediately understand where this was going. They have already experienced political stagnancy, collective isolation and emotional unavailability, dissociated online identities, violent cults and doggish devotion to capital, it just took a while for the rest of the world to follow suit.
So, my relationship to the internet is complicated. It's undeniably formulated so much of who I am and what I like. I've found deep friendships and social groups where I feel like I belong, and people understand me. It's allowed me to stay connected with friends who moved away. It's been a safe haven when real life was too miserable and lonely, when I felt trapped or my real life relationships with my family were too painful to deal with. But ultimately the internet does not need me, yet I am parasitically linked to it. I have constructed a whole lived memory of being online, and I am scared now because the internet and reality seem to have devoured the other. The overarching parts of the internet that I thought were static, unchanging bastions no longer exist, or never mattered. People are impermeable, disappearing with no fanfare, without a trace. The world has been engulfed, everyone is online, at a time when it is the most ugly and predatory. Let's all love Lain. I have one remaining pocket that continues to bring me happiness, but now I frantically spend a majority of my time thinking about how I can make that pocket exist for me in real life for more than just a couple times a year. I have been living a simulacrum of human, emotional sustenance. _Fleurs du mal._ Artificial paradises and illusory comforts. How do I make a life in the Real, when I have let my senses wither and deteriorate my whole life? I can't simply log off, and pretend that none of this is a part of me. I tried that once, I could not be understood. I did not understand. But I can't just stay here. I want to learn to be honest. I want to be genuine. I want to learn how to be seen. I want to see others. Selfishly, I want to bring the love I know with me, to the Real. Would they recognize me? Would I recognize them? Would I find out it was it all just facsimile, preserved in code, crumbling as it meets the oxygen in the atmosphere? Please, tell me how to resolve these contradictory parts of my being. I have learned to love where there is no flesh to reciprocate it. I have let my flesh turn cold, where bodies lay dead around me. _The beginning comes, whether you like it or not, but the end does not come by itself. It is you who must find it._ Once again, I retreat. How much longer? I'm still searching for the Real, but what does that even mean? I think I've made it up.
